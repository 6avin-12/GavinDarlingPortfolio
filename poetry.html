In the Hills of Maryland

His fingers dance
on the steering wheel
flying through the hills
of Maryland.

In the backseat we obey inertia
so you fall on me
going deeper in the hills
of Maryland.

I catch every ray of sun through your hair
but drive faster still

because
when this car stops,
you'll leave me here
in Maryland.

My love please don't cry.
We'll meet again
in the hills
of Maryland.

I forgot to feed my worms

Inside me
grows parasitic worms.
Writhing in my stomach
and sticking like black tar,
they squirm their way up my throat
seeking a little more.
They fill every space,
violating every hole and crevice.
They find my nasal cavity.
and then my eye sockets.
I feel them from the inside
squeezing around the corner of my eyes
until worms bleed out of every oriface.
no longer my own.
My carcass is deserted now,
but the worms grow hungrier still.
