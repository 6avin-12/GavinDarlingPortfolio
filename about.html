Autobiography


I was born on the bayfront of Erie, Pennsylvania. Until three, I would not speak a word and struggled with speaking throughout my childhood. Instead, I found solace in my imagination and dreamt of turning into a dragon to fly away.

Obsessed with drawing mythological creatures, I became somewhat skilled in art at a young age. This inspired me to develop skills in other mediums like painting. Selling my first painting in a coffee shop at 14, I naturally pursued a career in art and design.

In my free time, I enjoy playing guitar, making music, designing websites in HTML and CSS, writing poetry, drawing, photography, and challenging myself on long bike rides and hikes. Being in nature and playing guitar in particular have been instrumental against my continuous battle with my addictions.

I excelled in art, math, and writing in school – art predictably being my favorite subject. By high school, I would take every art class available. I also became increasingly invested in computer classes, science, and social science. To pursue my interests, I took advanced placement classes in art, psychology, government, biology, and computer science. Now, I am studying multimedia and graphic design at Point Park University in Pittsburgh.

Through the influence of media like art, photography, and video, I aspire to design and create messages for non-profit organizations to campaign for issues related to health. The significance of health is not lost on me as the son of a therapist and dentist. Addiction, environmental health, and education are particularly significant issues to me as an American.
